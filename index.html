MultiSwap: Cross-Modal Identity Conversion with Silent Video and Audio Inputs

Yifan Liu1, Yu Fang1, Chao Zhang2,3, Zhouhan Lin1

1 Shanghai Jiao Tong University   |   2 Shanghai AI Lab   |   3 Tsinghua University

Abstract

Face-based Voice Conversion (FVC) leverages facial representations to modify speaker identity, using source speech to provide content while ensuring the generated speech matches the target identity inferred from facial images. While facial identity is derived from visual input, the content is traditionally taken from speech, creating an audio-visual gap between content and identity. To address this challenge, we propose MultiSwap, a novel framework that extends FVC by integrating both audio and visual modalities for the content input, which handles three distinct sub-tasks: V+I→A (silent video with identity image to speech), A+I→A (audio and identity image to speech), and AV+I→A (combined audio-visual and identity image to speech).

We introduce task-specific models for each sub-task as well as a unified solution that can handle all three tasks with a single model. Furthermore, we present a noise-robust variant of MultiSwap that outperforms prior FVC methods in content recovery under noisy conditions. In all tasks, MultiSwap achieves strong performance in speech conversion and synthesis, as measured by both objective and subjective metrics, demonstrating its potential for multi-modal FVC applications.
